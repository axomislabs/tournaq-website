The KO System is a classic single-elimination knockout bracket. Teams are drawn into a bracket before the tournament starts, and each match is decisive — the winner advances, the loser is eliminated. The bracket progresses round by round until one team remains. TournaQ handles the full bracket setup, match progression, and result recording automatically.

How it works

The organiser sets up the bracket before play begins — choosing the number of teams, seeding method (random draw or manual seeding), and whether a third-place match is played. TournaQ generates the full bracket and assigns first-round match-ups. As results are entered, the bracket updates automatically and the next round is populated. The final match produces an outright winner.

Setup options

  • Number of teams: 4, 8, 16, or 32 (power-of-two draws)
  • Seeding: random draw or manual placement
  • Third-place play-off: optional bronze medal match
  • Match format: configurable sets and points per match
  • Byes: automatically assigned for non-power-of-two team counts

Best for

  • Competitive events where a clear winner is the goal
  • Tournaments following a pool phase — KO rounds from the top teams
  • Events where spectators and players can easily follow the bracket
  • Any format where high-stakes single-elimination drama is part of the appeal
