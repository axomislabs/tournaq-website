Doghouse is a continuous play format for groups where not everyone can be on court at the same time. The losing team steps off and joins the back of a waiting queue — the "doghouse". The next team in the queue steps straight on to face the winners. Play never stops, and teams rotate through the queue until time runs out or a target win count is reached.

How it works

The organiser registers all teams and sets the match format (typically a single short set to keep the queue moving). TournaQ tracks the queue order, records each result, and updates standings in real time. The team with the most wins when the session ends is the winner. Matches are intentionally short — the energy comes from the constant rotation and the pressure of defending on court.

Best for

  • Club training sessions with an odd number of teams
  • Social evenings where continuous action is the priority
  • Groups of 3–6 teams on a single court
  • Warm-up competitions before a main draw tournament
